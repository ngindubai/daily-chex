Daily Chex Operating Manual

Complete operating instructions for the Daily Chex safety inspection platform. Covers all features for operators, supervisors, managers, and administrators.

Version 1.0 — Updated July 2025

Daily Chex is a digital safety inspection system for construction plant, vehicles, and trailers. Field operatives use QR codes and mobile devices to perform structured safety checks, while managers maintain oversight through real-time dashboards, GPS tracking, and task assignments.

Logging In

There are two login methods depending on your role.

PIN Login (Operators & Supervisors)

Operators and supervisors log in using a 4-digit PIN code. This is designed for fast access on shared site devices.

1

Select Company

Choose your company from the dropdown on the login screen.

2

Tap "Enter PIN"

A numeric keypad appears with 4 visual dots for each digit.

3

Enter Your 4-Digit PIN

The system automatically submits when you tap the 4th digit. If your PIN is correct, you'll be taken to the dashboard.

💡
Tip: Your PIN is unique across the company. If you forget your PIN, ask your manager to look it up or reset it in the People section.

Email Login (Managers & Admins)

Managers and administrators log in with their email address and password.

1

Tap "Manager Login"

Switch to the email/password login form.

2

Enter Email & Password

Type your registered email address and password, then tap Sign In.

⚠
Session: Your login session is stored on the device. You'll stay logged in until you tap the logout button or clear your browser data.

Dashboard

The dashboard is your home screen. It shows different information depending on your role.

Operator Dashboard

Operators see a simplified home designed for fast action:

Operator Home Screen
  • Scan QR Code — large button at the top. Tap to open the camera and scan an asset's QR code to start a check.
  • Assigned Tasks — if your manager has assigned checks to you, they appear here with priority indicators and due dates. Tap a task to start the check immediately.
  • Your Recent Checks — your last 5 checks with pass/fail status and time ago.
  • Quick Stats — total assets and open defects count.

Manager Dashboard

Managers and supervisors see the full command overview:

Manager Dashboard Features
  • Stats Grid — total assets, checks completed today, open defects (with critical count), and active crew numbers.
  • Map Card — preview card showing GPS check count. Tap to open the full interactive map.
  • Recent Checks — the 5 most recent checks across all operators, with pass/fail status and operator name.
  • Site Overview — each site shows team count, asset count, today's check progress (as a percentage bar), and open defect count.
  • Defect Breakdown — severity distribution (critical, high, medium, low) with color-coded badges. Pulsing red badge for critical defects.
  • Calibration Warnings — assets with calibration due within 14 days, showing days remaining.

QR Scanning

Every asset in the system has a unique QR code (format: CHX-XXXXXXXX). Scanning is the fastest way to start a check.

1

Open the Scanner

Tap Scan in the navigation bar or the Scan QR Code button on your dashboard.

2

Point at the QR Code

The camera opens automatically. Hold your device steady and aim at the QR label on the asset. The scanner reads the code instantly.

3

Asset Found

The system looks up the asset and takes you directly to the check flow with that asset pre-selected.

Manual Code Entry

If the QR label is damaged or your camera isn't working, you can type the code manually:

  1. On the Scan page, tap Enter code manually.
  2. Type the full code (e.g., CHX-A2B4C6D8).
  3. Tap Look Up to find the asset.
💡
Tip: You can also start a check without any QR code at all. Go to Checks → New Check and search for the asset by name, registration, or plant ID.

Performing a Check

A check is a structured safety inspection of an asset. The check flow has 5 steps. GPS location is captured at the start and end of each check.

Step 1: Select Asset

Search for the asset by name, registration number, or plant ID. The list shows all active assets in your company. If you arrived here from a QR scan or task assignment, the asset is already selected.

Each asset card shows its type (vehicle, trailer, or plant), registration/plant ID, and current status.

Step 2: Select Check Template

Choose which check template to apply. Templates are filtered to match the asset type (e.g., "Vehicle Daily Check" only shows for vehicles). Common templates include:

  • Daily Check — Short inspection covering essential safety items
  • Weekly Check — More thorough inspection with additional items

Some check items only appear based on asset properties (e.g., additional brake checks for vehicles over 7.5 tonnes).

Step 3: Complete the Checklist

This is the main inspection step

Items are grouped by section (e.g., General, Engine, Brakes, Lights). For each item:

  • ✓ Pass (green) — the item is satisfactory
  • ✗ Fail (red) — the item has a defect
  • — N/A — the item doesn't apply to this asset

You can add optional notes to any item by tapping the notes field below it. Notes are especially useful for failed items to describe what's wrong.

📍
GPS Location: When you start the checklist, the app captures your GPS position. Make sure you allow location access when prompted. This verifies you were physically at the asset location.

Step 4: Summary

The summary screen shows your inspection result:

  • PASS — All items passed. The asset is safe to use.
  • FAIL — One or more items failed. Failed items are listed. Defects will be created automatically.

You can optionally record mileage readings (start/end) on this screen.

Step 5: Sign & Submit

Final step — your digital signature

Use your finger or stylus to sign in the signature pad. This confirms you performed the inspection honestly and accurately.

Tap Submit Check to save. The system will:

  • Save the check record with all item results
  • Capture your GPS location (end position)
  • Create defect reports for any failed items
  • If this check was from a task assignment, mark the task as completed
  • If any critical defect is found (brakes, steering, fire equipment), the asset is automatically flagged as Defective
⚠
Important: Once submitted, a check cannot be edited. Make sure all items are correctly marked before signing. If you made a mistake, you will need to perform a new check.

Task Assignments

Managers and supervisors can create check assignments for operators. Tasks specify which asset needs checking, which template to use, who should do it, and when it's due.

Creating Tasks Manager Supervisor

1

Go to Tasks Page

Navigate to Tasks in the sidebar. The create form appears at the top.

2

Select Asset & Template

Choose the asset that needs inspecting. The template dropdown auto-filters to show only templates matching that asset type.

3

Assign To

Select the person or team who should perform the check. Assigning to a team means any member can pick it up.

4

Set Priority & Due Date

Choose a priority level and optionally set a due date. Tap Create Task to assign it.

Priority Levels

Completing Tasks Operator

How Operators Complete Assigned Tasks
  1. Open the Dashboard — pending tasks show in the "Assigned Tasks" section
  2. Tap a task to start. The check flow opens with the asset and template pre-selected.
  3. Complete the check normally (checklist → summary → sign).
  4. The task automatically updates to Completed when you submit the check.

Defect Management

Defects are problems found during safety checks. They can be created automatically when a check item fails, or manually by supervisors and managers.

Auto-Created Defects

When a check is submitted with failed items, the system automatically creates a defect for each failure:

Automatic Severity Assignment
Failed Item ContainsAuto SeverityAsset Impact
Brake, steering, or fire equipmentCriticalAsset marked defective — must not be used
Tyre, light, or exhaustHighAsset remains active but needs attention
Any other itemMediumAsset remains active

Defect Lifecycle

1

Open

Defect is reported (auto or manual). Appears in the defects list with severity badge.

2

In Progress

Someone is working on the fix. Updated via the defect detail page.

3

Resolved

Fix verified. Record action taken, who resolved it, and when.

Defect Detail Page

Open any defect to view full details and take action:

  • Status controls — change status (open → in progress → resolved)
  • Action taken — text field to describe the repair or fix
  • Resolved by — records who fixed the issue
  • Linked check — if auto-created, links back to the original check
⏰
Overdue Alerts: Defects that remain open or in-progress get visual warnings: amber ring after 24 hours, red ring after 72 hours. These appear in both the defect list and dashboard.

Creating a New Asset Manager

  1. Go to Assets and tap Add Asset.
  2. Select the asset type (Vehicle / Trailer / Plant).
  3. Enter the name, and optionally the registration, plant ID, or serial number.
  4. Choose the weight class if applicable (affects check templates for vehicles).
  5. Set the site assignment and any maintenance dates.
  6. Tap Create. A unique QR code is generated automatically.

QR Code Labels

Each asset gets a unique QR code in the format CHX-XXXXXXXX (8 characters). Print labels from the Asset Detail page.

Printing QR Labels
  1. Open the asset you want to label.
  2. In the asset detail page, you'll see the QR code preview.
  3. For bulk printing, go to Assets → QR Labels.
  4. Labels are generated in a printable format (3 per row) showing the asset name, QR image, and ID.
💡
Tip: Print QR labels on weatherproof sticker paper and apply them to a visible, protected spot on the asset. Common locations: cab doors for vehicles, A-frames for trailers, and control panels for plant.

Asset Transfers

Move assets between sites to track current deployment. Open the asset detail page, tap Transfer, and select the new site. The transfer is logged with a timestamp.

Sites, Teams & People

The organisational hierarchy is: Company → Sites → Teams → People.

Sites

Construction sites or work locations. Each site has:

  • Name & address (e.g., "M62 Junction 8 Upgrade, Burtonwood, WA5 4QE")
  • GPS coordinates (latitude/longitude) for map display
  • Teams assigned to the site
  • Assets deployed at the site

Teams

Groups of people working at a site. Each team has a name (e.g., "Alpha Crew") and an optional team leader. Tasks can be assigned to entire teams.

People Management Manager

Adding a New Person
  1. Go to People and tap Add Person.
  2. Enter first name, last name, and role.
  3. For operators/supervisors: set a 4-digit PIN (this is their login code).
  4. For managers/admins: set an email address and password.
  5. Optionally assign to a team, add phone number, email, and licence number.
  6. Tap Create.
⚠
PIN Security: PINs are hashed and cannot be viewed after creation. If an operator forgets their PIN, a manager must set a new one. Do not reuse PINs across different people.

Map & GPS Tracking

The interactive map shows where safety checks have been performed, using GPS coordinates captured during each check.

Map Features
  • Colored markers show check locations:
    • Green — Check passed
    • Red — Check failed (defects found)
    • Yellow (pulsing) — Check in progress / very recent (within 5 minutes)
  • Marker popups — tap any marker to see asset name, operator name, check result, and time.
  • Auto-zoom — the map automatically fits all visible markers in view.
  • Dark theme — uses high-contrast dark map tiles matching the app theme.
📍
GPS Accuracy: GPS coordinates are captured with the device's best available accuracy. For best results, ensure location services are enabled and you're in an area with good GPS signal. Indoor locations may have reduced accuracy.

Check History

The Checks page shows a complete history of all inspections performed.

Checks Page Features
  • Search — find checks by asset name or operator name
  • Filter by result — show only passes, fails, or all
  • Check details — tap any check to see the full inspection record, including:
    • Every checklist item and its result (pass/fail/N/A)
    • Notes added to specific items
    • GPS coordinates (start and end)
    • Mileage readings (if entered)
    • Digital signature image
    • Linked defects (if any)
    • Timestamps
  • New Check button — start a check without scanning (manual asset selection)

Check Templates

Templates define the checklist questions for each type of inspection. They are pre-configured by the system and matched to asset types automatically.

Template Structure
  • Name — e.g., "Vehicle Daily Check", "Plant Weekly Inspection"
  • Asset Type — vehicle, trailer, or plant (only matching templates are shown)
  • Frequency — daily or weekly
  • Sections — checklist items grouped by category (General, Engine, Brakes, Lights, etc.)
  • Conditional Items — some items only appear based on asset properties. For example, additional brake checks appear only for vehicles classed as "over 7.5 tonnes".

Troubleshooting

Common Issues
ProblemSolution
QR scanner won't open Check that your browser has camera permission. Go to browser settings → Site settings → Camera, and allow access for the Daily Chex URL.
PIN login rejected Make sure you selected the correct company. PINs are company-specific. If you've forgotten your PIN, ask your manager to reset it.
GPS location not captured Enable Location Services on your device. Check browser permissions. GPS works best outdoors with clear sky. Indoor locations may not capture coordinates.
Check won't submit All required checklist items must be answered (pass, fail, or N/A). Check that you haven't missed any items. The submit button is disabled until the signature is provided.
"Session expired" error Your login token has expired. Log out and log back in. This is more common for manager accounts (24-hour session limit).
Asset shows as "Defective" A critical defect (brakes/steering/fire equipment) was found. The asset cannot be used until the defect is resolved and the status is manually changed back to "Active" by a manager.
Wrong check template showing Templates filter by asset type. If you selected a vehicle but see plant templates, the asset may be miscategorised. Ask a manager to check the asset type in Asset Management.
Can't see assigned tasks Tasks appear on the Dashboard and Tasks page. Make sure the task was assigned to you personally (not just your team). Check with your supervisor.
Map shows no markers GPS must have been captured during checks. If all checks were done indoors without GPS signal, no markers will appear. Recent checks (last 24h) with GPS are shown.
