Chex

Digital plant & vehicle check tracking for tradesmen

Scoping Document · v1.0 · 3 April 2026
Status: Awaiting Client Approval

The Problem Today

Right now, your teams fill in three separate paper forms every week by hand. These track the condition of vehicles, trailers, and plant equipment across your sites. The forms are:

1. Vehicle & Trailer Inspection Record

MGroup Services format

A weekly grid where drivers tick off 15 daily checks on their vehicle (oil, tyres, mirrors, lights, brakes, etc.) plus another 15 checks on any attached trailer (towing cable, breakaway cables, wheel nuts, securing straps, etc.). Includes a defects section and sign-off.

2. Weekly PUWER Inspection Report

SAW Ltd format

Tracks all plant and equipment on site — diggers, dumpers, generators, breakers, saws, work lights, etc. Records the supplier, the Plant ID number, daily pass/fail checks (Mon–Sun), any defects found and actions taken, calibration dates, and who checked it.

3. Vehicle Daily Check Sheet

General fleet format

The most detailed vehicle form — covers general roadworthiness (fuel/oil/water, battery, tyres, windscreen, washers, mirrors, lights, body damage), plus sections for vehicles over 7.5T (tachograph, plateing certificate, speed limiter, crane operation) and towing/lifting equipment. Includes mileage tracking, defect reporting, and driver/agent sign-off.

What goes wrong with paper

  • Forms get lost, damaged, or left in vans — nobody back at the office knows the true status of kit on site.
  • Handwriting is hard to read — serial numbers, Plant IDs, and defect notes get misread or missed entirely.
  • No single view — to see the status of all your plant, someone has to physically collect and collate three different paper sheets per site, per week.
  • Compliance risk — if an inspector asks for records, you're rummaging through filing cabinets. Missing or incomplete forms are a liability.
  • Duplicate effort — some information (vehicle reg, site name, operator name) is written out on every form, every week.

The Proposed Solution

Chex is a web app that replaces all three paper forms with a single digital tool. It runs on any phone, tablet, or laptop — no app store download needed. Here's what it does:

One app, three forms, zero paper

Each of the three paper forms becomes a digital checklist inside Chex. Your teams open the app on their phone, pick the check they need to do, and tap through it. Everything is saved instantly.

How a typical day works

1
Scan the QR code

Every vehicle, trailer, and piece of plant gets a QR code sticker. The operator scans it with their phone camera to pull up that item instantly — no typing serial numbers.

2
Run through the checklist

The app shows the right checklist for that item (vehicle checks, trailer checks, or plant/PUWER checks). Tap pass, fail, or N/A for each item. It takes a fraction of the time compared to writing it all out.

3
Snap a photo if there's a defect

Found damage? Take a photo straight from the app. It's attached to the record automatically — no need to describe dents and scratches in words.

4
Sign off digitally

The operator confirms with a digital signature. The record is timestamped and stored securely.

5
Office sees everything in real time

Back at the office, managers can see the status of every vehicle and piece of plant across all sites — live, in one place. No waiting for paper to come back.

Key Features

📋 Digital Checklists

All three paper forms rebuilt as tap-through checklists. Each item on the form becomes a pass/fail/N/A button. Defect notes can be typed or spoken. Covers:

  • Vehicle daily safety inspection (15 items + defects)
  • Trailer daily safety inspection (15 items + defects)
  • PUWER weekly plant inspection (per item, with supplier and Plant ID)
  • Vehicle roadworthiness check (general, 7.5T+, towing/lifting)
  • Mileage tracking (start/end of week)

📷 QR Codes & Camera

  • QR code scanning — scan a sticker on any piece of kit to instantly pull up its record and start a check.
  • QR code generation — the app creates printable QR stickers for new vehicles/plant.
  • Photo capture — take photos of damage, defects, or anything that needs recording. Photos are attached to the check record.

📊 Live Dashboard

  • See all vehicles, trailers, and plant in one place.
  • Filter by site, by date, by status (passed / has defects / overdue).
  • Spot equipment with overdue checks or unresolved defects at a glance.
  • Track calibration/test dates and get reminders before they're due.

📄 PDF Extracts

Need to produce the original paper forms for compliance, audits, or client requests? Chex generates PDF versions that match the familiar layouts — filled in with all the recorded data. Print them or email them.

🔌 API for Back-Office Integration

An open API so Chex data can flow into your existing systems — accounting, fleet management, hire-desk software, or merchant back-ends. No double-entry.

Technical Approach

Keeping this brief — the detail matters less than the outcome, but for the record:

  • Web app — works in any browser, no app store needed. Can be saved to the home screen on a phone so it looks and feels like a native app.
  • Camera access — uses the phone's built-in camera for QR scanning and photos. No extra hardware.
  • Works offline — checks can be started even with poor signal on site. Data syncs when connection is restored.
  • Secure — data is encrypted, users log in with their own account, and access is controlled by role.
  • Hosted in the cloud — no servers to manage on your end. Automatic backups.
