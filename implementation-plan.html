Complete technical design for Phase 1 build

v1.0 · 3 April 2026 · Internal working document

1. Organisational Hierarchy

The client runs multiple teams across multiple sites. A single site can have several teams working simultaneously. The data model needs to reflect this reality from day one — retrofitting hierarchy is painful.

The hierarchy

Company (tenant)
  └─ Sites (job sites / locations)
       └─ Teams (gangs / crews assigned to a site)
            └─ People (individual operators / drivers)
                 └─ Assets (vehicles, trailers, plant)
                      └─ Checks (inspections performed on assets)

Key rules

  • A company is the top-level tenant. Everything is scoped to a company. In v1, we build for a single company, but the schema supports multi-tenancy from the start so the app can be resold.
  • A site is a physical job location (e.g. "M6 Junction 19 Resurfacing", "Stockport Town Centre Drainage"). Sites can be active or archived. A site has a name, optional address/postcode, and optional GPS coordinates.
  • A team belongs to a site. Multiple teams can be on one site (e.g. "Day Shift", "Night Crew", "Subcontractor — ABC Ltd"). A team has a name, a team leader reference, and a site assignment. Teams can be moved between sites.
  • A person is an individual operator, driver, or supervisor. A person belongs to a company and can be assigned to one team at a time (but can be reassigned). They have a name, phone, optional email, role, and optional operator licence number. They can also be unassigned (floaters, office staff).
  • An asset (vehicle / trailer / plant) is owned by the company and currently assigned to a site. Assets move between sites as jobs change. An asset's check history follows it regardless of which site it was at when checked.
Design decision: Teams are lightweight. They're essentially named groups of people at a site. We don't over-engineer them — no sub-teams, no nested hierarchies. If a foreman wants to see "his lads' checks", he filters by team. If the office wants all checks for a site, they filter by site. Simple.
Nuance: When a person does a check, we record which site and team they were assigned to at that moment. If they later move to a different team, the historical check still shows the original assignment. This matters for audit trails.

4. Authentication & Roles

Tradesmen don't want to remember passwords. They're on a cold site at 6am in work gloves.

Auth approach: PIN + device trust

  • Operators/drivers log in with a 4-digit PIN. No email required. The manager creates their account and gives them a PIN. The PIN is scoped to the company (two companies could have the same PIN for different people, but within a company, PINs are unique).
  • Supervisors and Managers log in with email + password for dashboard access. They can also use a PIN for quick on-site access.
  • Device trust: after first login on a device, we store a secure token. The operator just enters their PIN next time — no username step. The device "knows" which company it belongs to.
  • Session length: 30 days for operators (they shouldn't have to log in every day), 24 hours for dashboard sessions.

5. QR Code System

QR generation

  • Each asset gets a unique QR code when created. Format: CHX-{8-char-alphanumeric} (e.g. CHX-A7K9M2P4).
  • The QR encodes a URL: https://app.chex.io/q/{CHX-A7K9M2P4}. This means any phone can scan it — no app needed. The URL resolves to the asset's check page.
  • QR stickers are generated as downloadable PDFs — sized for standard label printers (62mm × 30mm fits a Brother QL label). Include the Chex logo, asset name, QR code, and the ID in human-readable text.
  • Batch print: managers can select multiple assets and generate a sheet of QR stickers.

QR scanning flow

  1. Operator opens Chex → taps "Scan" (or just visits the QR URL directly from their phone camera).
  2. Camera opens, operator points at the sticker.
  3. App looks up the asset by QR code.
  4. If the operator is logged in → shows the asset and prompts "Start check?"
  5. If not logged in → prompts for PIN first, then shows the asset.
  6. If the QR code is unknown → shows "Asset not found. Ask your manager to register this item."
Offline consideration: QR lookup needs to work offline. The PWA caches the asset register locally. The QR code is matched against the local cache. If no match (new asset not yet synced), it queues the check to be linked when connectivity returns.

6. Check Flow — Step by Step

What happens from QR scan to completed check record.

Daily vehicle check (Template 3) — most detailed flow

  1. Scan or select asset → app identifies the vehicle, shows its name, reg, last check date.
  2. Confirm identity → "Checking as: John Smith, Team: Alpha Crew, Site: M6 Jcn 19". If wrong, operator can switch (but can only switch to teams they're assigned to).
  3. Mileage entry → if it's the start of the week, prompt for start mileage. If end of week, prompt for end mileage. Auto-populate from last check's end mileage where available.
  4. Checklist — General Roadworthiness → 20 items shown as a scrollable list. Each item: tap ✓ (pass), ✗ (fail), or "N/A". Failed items expand to show a notes field and camera button.
  5. Checklist — 7.5T+ section → only shown if the vehicle's weight class is over 7.5T. 6 additional items.
  6. Checklist — Towing/Lifting → only shown if the vehicle has towing/lifting capability (asset flag). 4 items.
  7. Defect summary → if any items failed, shows a summary screen. Operator must describe each defect. Can take photos. Defect severity auto-suggested (critical if brakes/steering, low if bodywork).
  8. Confirmation → "You checked 26 items: 24 pass, 2 fail, 0 N/A. 2 defects reported." Shows a clear pass/fail indicator.
  9. Signature → operator draws their signature on screen. Timestamped.
  10. Submit → record saved. If online, synced immediately. If offline, queued.
  11. Agent countersign → optional. If the supervisor is present, they can countersign immediately. Otherwise, the check shows "Awaiting agent signature" in the dashboard.

Weekly PUWER check (Template 2) — different flow

  1. Supervisor opens "PUWER Weekly" from the site dashboard (not QR-triggered — this is a site-level activity).
  2. Shows all plant assigned to this site — listed with supplier, plant ID, category. The supervisor can add/remove assets from the site register here.
  3. Daily grid → for each day of the current week, tap ✓ or ✗ per asset. This is the familiar paper grid experience. The app pre-fills today's column and makes it easy to go back and fill previous days.
  4. Defect notes → tap any ✗ to expand and add defect details, photos, action taken.
  5. Calibration dates → shown alongside each asset. Highlighted amber if due within 30 days, red if overdue.
  6. Sign off → supervisor signs the whole weekly report.

7. Dashboard Views

Manager dashboard (all sites)

  • Overview cards: Total assets, checks completed today, open defects, overdue checks, assets needing calibration.
  • Site list: Each site shows: name, team count, asset count, today's check completion rate (e.g. "14/18 checks done"), defect count.
  • Filter bar: By site, by date range, by status (all / has defects / overdue / complete).
  • Asset register: Full list of all assets across all sites, with current status, last check date, current site assignment.
  • People directory: All operators, their team assignment, last check performed, total checks this week.

Supervisor dashboard (single site)

  • Sees only assets and people at their site.
  • Team view: Filter by team to see only that team's checks and assets.
  • Today's outstanding: Which assets haven't been checked yet today? Which operators haven't done their checks?
  • Defect queue: Open defects needing resolution or escalation.
  • PUWER grid: The weekly plant inspection view, same as the paper form layout.

Operator view (their own work)

  • Main screen: "Scan QR" button (big, prominent), recent checks, any assigned assets needing checks today.
  • My checks: History of all checks they've performed.
  • My defects: Defects they've reported and their resolution status.

8. Site & Team Management — Detail

This is where the nuance lives. The client has several teams per site and needs to track who did what, where, and when.

Creating and managing sites

  • Manager goes to Sites → Add Site.
  • Fields: Site Name (required), Address/Postcode (optional), GPS pin (optional — device can auto-detect).
  • Site is immediately active. When a job ends, the manager archives the site. Archived sites and their data remain in the system but don't appear in active filters.
  • Archiving a site does not delete team assignments or asset assignments — it preserves them as historical record. But assets at an archived site are flagged for reassignment.

Creating and managing teams

  • Within a site, the manager creates teams: Sites → [Site Name] → Teams → Add Team.
  • Fields: Team Name (required), Team Leader (optional — select from people at this site).
  • Examples: "Day Shift", "Night Crew", "Groundworks Gang", "Sub — ABC Ltd", "Traffic Management".
  • Teams can be renamed, archived, or moved to a different site (the whole team moves, including members).
  • A person can only be on one team at a time. Reassigning them to a new team removes them from the old one.

Assigning people

  • From the people directory: Select a person → Assign to Site → Choose Team. One action does both.
  • From the team view: Add Member → pick from existing people or create a new person inline.
  • Unassigned people (no team) can still perform checks — they just won't appear under any team filter. This handles office staff or floaters who visit multiple sites.
  • Quick reassign: Drag or tap to move a person between teams within the same site. Cross-site transfer available from the person detail screen.
Why this matters: When the office gets a defect report from "M6 Junction 19, Day Shift, John Smith", they instantly know the site, the crew responsible, and the individual. When they need all checks for a specific team (e.g. for a client audit), they filter by team. When an insurance company asks for a specific vehicle's history, they filter by asset — the team and site context travels with the record.

Asset assignment to sites

  • Assets belong to the company but are assigned to a site at any given time.
  • Move an asset between sites: Asset Detail → Transfer to Site → [select site]. The transfer is timestamped and logged.
  • Assets can be "unassigned" — in the yard, off-hire, or in for repair. They show in the global asset register but not under any site.
  • When an asset moves sites, its full check history goes with it. The history shows which site each check was performed at.

9. Offline / PWA Strategy

Reality: construction sites have bad signal

The app must work offline. Not "degrade gracefully" — actually work. An operator at the bottom of a trench needs to do their check regardless of signal.

  • Service worker caches the entire app shell, all templates, and the asset register for the current site.
  • IndexedDB stores the local asset register, recent checks, and any queued (unsynced) data.
  • QR lookup works offline — matches against the locally cached asset register.
  • Checks are saved locally first, always. If online, they sync immediately. If offline, they queue and sync when connectivity returns.
  • Conflict resolution: Last-write-wins for most fields. For defect resolution specifically, server-side wins (the office's update takes precedence over a stale local state).
  • Sync indicator: A small badge in the app header shows "2 checks pending sync" so the operator knows data is queued.
  • Photo handling: Photos are compressed client-side (max 1200px, ~200KB) and stored in IndexedDB until sync. On sync, they upload to object storage and the URL is written to the record.

13. Nuances & Edge Cases

The things that break apps in the real world.

Things we have to handle

ScenarioHow we handle it
Operator starts a check, phone dies mid-wayCheck saved in in_progress state. When they reopen the app, it offers to resume. Auto-saved every few seconds to IndexedDB.
Two operators scan the same asset at the same timeBoth create separate check records. The dashboard flags duplicate checks for the same asset on the same day. Supervisor resolves which one counts.
Asset is moved to a new site mid-week during a PUWER checkThe PUWER check keeps running at the original site for that week. Next week, the asset appears at the new site's PUWER grid.
New asset arrives on site, no QR sticker yetOperator selects "Add new asset" from the app. Fills in details manually. QR sticker can be printed later. The asset is immediately available for checks.
Operator forgets their PINSupervisor can reset it from the dashboard. Operator gets a new PIN immediately.
Check completed offline, then connectivity returns while away from siteCheck syncs with the site/team assignment that was current at the time of the check, not the current assignment. Timestamps are device-local.
Vehicle reg changes (replaced vehicle on same contract)Old asset archived, new asset created. History stays separate. We don't overwrite.
Operator assigned to a team but isn't actually on site that dayNo issue — assignments are persistent, not daily attendance. If they don't check anything that day, the dashboard shows them as "no activity" but doesn't flag it as an error.
Calibration date has passed but plant is still in useAsset status doesn't auto-change. Dashboard shows a red "OVERDUE" badge. Manager gets a notification. It's their call whether to stand the kit down.
Defect marked as "critical" (brakes, steering)Asset auto-flags to defective status. Can't clear the flag until the defect is resolved by a supervisor+. Prevents the asset showing as "available" on the dashboard.
The same form layout is used by multiple companies, but with slightly different fieldsTemplates are versioned and can be company-specific or global. Custom fields can be added without breaking existing check records.
Inspector asks for records for a specific weekFilter by week_ending. All three form types for that week are returned. PDF generation (Phase 2) produces forms matching the original paper layout.

16. Open Questions

To confirm with the client before build

  1. Branding: Which of the 10 brand options do they want? This affects the entire UI theme.
  2. Domain: Do they want chex.io / chex.app / getchex.com, or something else? This affects QR URLs.
  3. Notification preferences: Do supervisors want SMS alerts for critical defects, or is the dashboard sufficient for v1?
  4. Agent countersignature: Is this always required, or optional? The paper form has both driver and agent signature lines.
  5. Multiple companies: Is this for one company only in v1, or should we expose multi-tenancy from the start?
  6. Hosting budget: This determines whether we go managed (Railway/Fly, ~$20/mo) or VPS (~$10/mo, more setup).
